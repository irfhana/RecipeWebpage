Flavours of SouthIndian Meals

A South Indian Full Meals experience is not just a culinary delight but also a celebration of the rich cultural heritage and diverse flavors of South India. The meticulous arrangement of dishes ensures a harmonious blend of tastes, textures, and nutritional elements, making it a feast for both the senses and the soul.

Preparation Time

  • Total: Approximately 2 hours
  • Preparation: 30 minutes
  • Cooking: 1 hour 30 minutes

Instructions

  • Rice:
    • Wash the rice thoroughly and cook it using your preferred method (steamed or pulao).

  • Sambar:
    • Cook toor dal until soft.
    • In a separate pot, prepare a tamarind pulp by soaking tamarind in warm water.
    • Boil the vegetables, add tamarind pulp, sambar powder, turmeric powder, and salt to taste.
    • In a pan, heat oil, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, asafoetida, curry leaves, and red/green chilies. Add this tempering to the sambar.
    • Garnish with coriander leaves.

  • Rasam:
    • Cook toor dal until soft.
    • In a pot, combine tamarind juice, tomatoes, cooked dal, rasam powder, salt, and turmeric powder.
    • In a pan, heat oil, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, black pepper, asafoetida, curry leaves, and chopped coriander leaves. Add this tempering to the rasam.
    • Boil for a few minutes and serve hot.

  • Poriyal (Stir-fried Vegetables):
    • Chop vegetables into small pieces.
    • In a pan, heat oil, add mustard seeds, urad dal, curry leaves, green chilies, and turmeric powder.
    • Add chopped vegetables, saut√© until cooked, and garnish with grated coconut.

  • Kootu:
    • Cook split yellow moong dal until soft.
    • Boil chopped vegetables with turmeric powder, salt, and water.
    • Grind coconut, green chilies, and cumin seeds to a paste.
    • Mix dal, boiled vegetables, and coconut paste. Season with mustard seeds, urad dal, and curry leaves.

  • Aviyal:
    • Boil mixed vegetables until cooked.
    • Grind coconut, green chilies, and cumin seeds to a coarse paste.
    • Mix vegetables, coconut paste, curd, and salt. Season with mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and curry leaves.

  • Curd (Yogurt):
    • Serve plain curd or whisked curd as it is.

  • Pickle:
    • Serve your choice of pickle.

  • Appalam (Papad):
    • Roast or fry the papads as per the instructions on the package.

  • Buttermilk (Mor):
    • Mix curd with water, add salt, and whisk well. Season with mustard seeds and curry leaves.

  • Sweets (Optional):
    • Serve a traditional South Indian sweet like payasam.

  • Serve:
    • Serve all the dishes in a traditional way banana leaf.
