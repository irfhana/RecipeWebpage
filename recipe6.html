Magical Blackcurrant Ice Cream Cake

The blackcurrant flavor in the ice cream is a symphony of sweet and tangy notes, creating a harmonious balance that tantalizes the taste buds. Each spoonful offers the intense essence of ripe blackcurrants, adding a burst of natural sweetness to the creamy, frozen texture.

Preparation Time

  • Total: Approximately 1 hour
  • Preparation: 15 minutes
  • Baking: 40 minutes

Ingredients

  • Blackcurrants (fresh or frozen)
  • Sugar
  • Heavy cream
  • Whole milk
  • Egg yolks
  • Vanilla extract (optional)
  • Lemon juice
  • Dark chocolate ganache (optional)
  • Crushed nuts (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Sponge cake layers or cookies
  • Blackcurrant syrup or jam (optional)
  • Fresh blackcurrants or other berries (for garnish)
  • Whipped cream (for garnish, optional)

Instructions

  • Prepare the Blackcurrant Ice Cream:
    • In a saucepan, combine blackcurrants and sugar. Cook over medium heat until the blackcurrants release their juices and become soft.
    • Blend the blackcurrant mixture until smooth. Strain to remove seeds, if desired.
    • In a separate saucepan, heat the milk and cream until just before boiling.
    • In a bowl, whisk the egg yolks. Gradually add the hot milk and cream mixture while continuously whisking.
    • Pour the egg and milk mixture back into the saucepan and cook over low heat, stirring constantly until it thickens to form a custard.
    • Allow the custard to cool slightly, then mix in the blackcurrant puree, vanilla extract (if using), and a splash of lemon juice for brightness.
    • Let the mixture cool completely before transferring it to an ice cream maker. Churn the mixture according to the manufacturer's instructions until it reaches a creamy consistency.
    • Transfer the blackcurrant ice cream to a container and freeze until it firms up.

  • Assemble the Ice Cream Cake:
    • If using a sponge cake, place one layer at the bottom of a springform pan or cake ring. If using cookies for a crust, crush them and press the crumbs into the bottom of the pan.
    • Pour a layer of blackcurrant ice cream over the cake or crust, spreading it evenly.
    • If desired, drizzle a layer of dark chocolate ganache and sprinkle crushed nuts.
    • Repeat the layers until you've used all the ice cream, finishing with a smooth top layer.
    • Freeze the cake for several hours or overnight until it's completely set.

  • Garnish and Serve:
    • Before serving, decorate the top with fresh blackcurrants, berries, and a dollop of whipped cream if desired.
    • Release the cake from the springform pan or cake ring, slice, and serve chilled.
