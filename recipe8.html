Nandu Varuval

This Nandu Varuval recipe results in a delightful dish where the succulent crab meat is infused with a medley of spices, creating a mouthwatering and aromatic experience.

Preparation Time

  • Total: Approximately 50 minutes
  • Preparation: 20 minutes
  • Cooking: 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • Fresh crabs
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Green chilies
  • Curry leaves
  • Mustard seeds
  • Turmeric powder
  • Red chili powder
  • Coriander powder
  • Garam masala
  • Fennel seeds (optional)
  • Oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

  • Cleaning and Preparing Crabs:
    • Begin by thoroughly cleaning and washing the fresh crabs. Remove the outer shell, gills, and any unwanted parts. Cut the crabs into manageable pieces.

  • Tempering:
    • Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds. Allow them to splutter.
    • Add curry leaves and finely chopped green chilies to infuse their flavors into the oil.

  • Aromatics:
    • Add finely chopped onions to the pan and saut√© until they turn golden brown.
    • Introduce ginger-garlic paste, stirring until the raw aroma diminishes.

  • Spices:
    • Sprinkle turmeric powder, red chili powder, and coriander powder into the mixture. Adjust the spice levels according to your preference.
    • Optionally, add fennel seeds for an extra layer of aromatic flavor.

  • Incorporating Tomatoes:
    • Add chopped tomatoes to the pan and cook until they soften and the oil starts separating from the masala.

  • Cooking Crab:
    • Carefully place the cleaned crab pieces into the masala, ensuring each piece is well-coated with the spice mixture.
    • Gently stir the crabs, allowing them to absorb the flavors of the masala.

  • Simmering to Perfection:
    • Cover the pan and let the crab cook in its own juices, allowing the flavors to permeate the seafood. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.

  • Garnishing and Serving:
    • Sprinkle garam masala and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
    • Serve the Nandu Varuval hot, accompanied by rice, dosa, or any other preferred Indian bread.
