Pav Bhaji Street Delight!

Indulge your senses in a burst of freshness with our Tropical Bliss Mixed Fruit Smoothie. Crafted to perfection, this vibrant concoction is a celebration of nature's sweetness and vitality, blending an assortment of luscious fruits into a symphony of flavors that dance on your taste buds.

Preparation Time

  • Total: Approximately 40 minutes
  • Preparation: 15 minutes
  • Cooking: 25 minutes

Ingredients

  • Potatoes, peas, carrots, and other vegetables, boiled and mashed
  • Butter
  • Capsicum (bell peppers) - finely chopped
  • Onions - finely chopped
  • Tomatoes - finely chopped
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Pav Bhaji masala
  • Red chili powder
  • Turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Lemon juice
  • Fresh coriander leaves - chopped
  • Pav (bread rolls)

Instructions

  • Prepare the Vegetables:
    • Boil potatoes, peas, and carrots until they are soft. Mash the potatoes and carrots.

  • Cooking the Bhaji:
    • In a large pan, melt butter.
    • Add finely chopped onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
    • Add finely chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate.
    • Stir in chopped capsicum and cook for a few minutes.

  • Spice it Up:
    • Add pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt to the vegetable mixture.
    • Mix well and let the spices cook for a couple of minutes.

  • Add Mashed Vegetables:
    • Add the mashed potatoes, peas, and carrots to the pan.
    • Mix everything thoroughly, ensuring that the vegetables are well-coated with the spices.

  • Simmer:
    • Allow the bhaji to simmer on low heat, stirring occasionally. This helps in blending the flavors.

  • Lemon Juice and Coriander:
    • Squeeze some lemon juice into the bhaji for a tangy flavor.
    • Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.

  • Prepare the Pav:
    • While the bhaji is simmering, slice the pav horizontally without separating the halves.
    • Heat a griddle or tawa, and toast the pav with a generous amount of butter until they turn golden brown.

  • Serve:
    • Serve the hot and flavorful bhaji with the buttered pav.
    • Optionally, garnish the bhaji with additional butter, chopped onions, and lemon wedges.
